Garlic Fries

These crispy fries coated with with the most delicious flavors will become a staple in your home. You will love how effortlessly they are to cook up. You can pair this up with almost any other dish of your liking, or just eat them alone. I hope you enjoy this amazing recipe. Happy cooking!

INGREDIENTS

POTATOES
  • 3 large Russet potaotes
  • 2 to 3 tbsp cornstarch
  • vegetable oil
BUTTER
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 tbsp parsley finley minced
  • 1 tbsp garlic finely minced
  • 2 to 3 tbsp parmesan grated
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp dries oregano
  • 1/2 tsp salt

INSTRUCTIONS

MAKE THE GARLIC BUTTER
  1. In a small bowl, add melted butter, parsley,garlic,parmesan,salt,black pepper,and oregano. Mix well and put to side.

    2. PREPARE THE POTATOES

  2. Rinse and peel the Russet potatoes
  3. Slice potatoes into desired length and thickness.
  4. Transfer over cut potatoes into an ice bath with a pinch some salt for at least 30 minutes.
  5. Boil some water in a pot and add potatoes in. Boil for 6 minutes we do not want them to be tender.
  6. Completely dry the fries, and add them to a tray or bowl. Sprinkle the cornstarch and shake until coated evenly.
  7. Heat oil in a frying pan over medium-low heat until the oil reaches about 250°F. You don't want to be the oil to be that hot for the first fry.
  8. Add the French fries and flash fry for 3 to 5 minutes. They will not be golden but they'll hold their shape well. Fry in batches so the pan is not over-crowded.
  9. Get the oil hotter now, about 350°F. Add the fries back in and fry until beautifully golden and crispy, making sure to move them around so they evenly brown and crisp up.
  10. Transfer to a wire rack to drain any excess oil. Sprinkle a little bit of salt.
  11. Add to a bowl along with the garlic butter. Toss around so the fries are coated evenly. Taste and adjust salt if needed.
  12. Enjoy!
